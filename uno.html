UNO RULES

These are the rules to be followed when playing uno with the French Living Learning Community Uno Club.

Game setup:

  1. The deck is shuffled and 7 cards are dealt to each player.
  2. The rest of the deck is placed face-down in the middle of the table.
  3. The top card of the deck is flipped face up onto the table. If a special card is flipped, it is shuffled into the deck and a new card is flipped.

Gameplay:

  1. The game proceeds in turns in clockwise order around the table, until the direction of play is changed by a reverse card.
  2. Each player plays a card as their onto the face-up pile that matches with either the colour or symbol of the card underneath. Black/multicoloured cards can be played as any colour. If a player cannot play during their turn, then they must pick up one card from the deck. If that card is playable, the player may play the card drawn.
  3. When a coloured card without a symbol is played, a number from 0-9 must be named. The card is then treated as if it were a card of the number named for as long as it remains in the pile.
  4. Cards of the same symbol can be played simultaneously. If multiple special cards are played, the effects of each card still apply.
  5. A +2 or +4 card usually skips the following player’s turn. However, if the following player also has a +2 or +4 card, (it must be the same, +2 cards cannot be matched with a +4 card) the following player may play their + card to push the effects on to the next player. The number of cards to be picked up accumulates until someone picks up.
  6. JUMPS on the top card, any card (must match colour and symbol). The players in between are skipped and the effect of only the card used to jump is applied.
  7. A player must call uno upon playing the second to last card in their hand. If another player calls uno first, the first player must pick up two cards from the deck.
  8. If a player empties their hand with a matching set of cards, the player must call the Spanish number equivalent to the number of cards played. If another player calls the correct number first, the first player must pick up a card and the game does not end yet.
